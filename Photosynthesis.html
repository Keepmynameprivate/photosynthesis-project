Photosynthesis: The Magic of Plants!

Learn how plants create food with sunlight, water, and air – it’s science at work!

What is Photosynthesis?

When you get hungry, you might decide to raid the cookie jar or ask someone to make you a sandwich. You do this because humans and animals get energy from the foods they eat. Plants, however, use light energy from the sun, combined with water and carbon dioxide, to produce the food they need to survive. This process is called photosynthesis.

When plants take in light from the sun, it powers a magical process. The sunlight travels into the plant and fuels a chemical reaction that allows the plant to create glucose (sugar) for energy. This energy helps the plant grow and survive. It’s like a plant’s secret power!

It's like magic – but it's science!

What Does a Plant Need?

During photosynthesis, light shining down from the sun is absorbed by the plant’s cells. Tiny cells are what make up the plant and its leaves. Inside these cells is a special energy-capturing substance called chlorophyll. Chlorophyll is green and it absorbs light energy, turning it into energy that can be used by the plant. The energy from the light causes a chemical reaction, and the leaf cells begin using “light” (photo) energy “to make” (synthesis) food energy.

Plants also need two other essential ingredients to complete the chemical reaction that creates their food energy. These ingredients are water and carbon dioxide, and they obtain water and carbon dioxide in two different ways. Plants absorb water through their roots and transport this water to their leaves via extremely narrow tubes called “xylem”. As these xylem bring water from the soil, “stomata” - tiny breathing holes on the underside of the leaves, absorb carbon dioxide from the air. With both of these important ingredients present, the plant can now complete the process of photosynthesis.

With sunlight, water, and CO2, the plant can start its magic!

The Chemical Reaction

As the light energy from the sun enters into the plant via the chlorophyll, a chemical reaction takes place that turns the light energy into food energy. During this chemical reaction, part of the water and carbon dioxide combine together to form a sugar the plant can use for food called “glucose”. This reaction also produces oxygen (which the plant doesn’t need), and it is released by the plant through the stomata. The sugar passes back into narrow tubes called the “phloem” and is transported throughout the plant where it can be stored and used as food energy.

Plants are our heroes! They make food and give us oxygen!

Why Is Photosynthesis Important?

Photosynthesis is a critical process that sustains life on Earth. It produces the oxygen we breathe and forms the foundation of the food chain by creating glucose, which serves as an energy source for both plants and animals. Plants are able to survive, grow, and reproduce because of photosynthesis, making it an essential process for life as we know it.

Photosynthesis is truly the magic of life!
